project background

This is a fictitious sample scenario used to demonstrate the generator. It does not describe
any real organisation or engagement.

The project is for the integration of an AI "Member Support Knowledge Copilot" into the staff
tooling of Northwind Building Society (a fictional mutual). The copilot is intended to support
contact-centre agents, branch staff and compliance reviewers by answering member-facing
questions with accurate, up-to-date information and the supporting source passage. It is
deployed alongside existing systems and aligned with the organisation's security and data
sovereignty requirements.

Problem statement

Member support involves a large, frequently changing body of product terms and conditions,
lending policies, complaint-handling procedures and regulatory guidance spread across
disconnected systems and intranets. Agents need fast, contextual, evidence-based answers while
they are on a call. The current requirement is to augment human decision-making without
compromising accuracy, compliance, or the consistency of what members are told.

intended users

contact-centre agents, branch staff and compliance/administrative reviewers

desired business outcome

Improve the speed and consistency of member query resolution, reduce agent onboarding time,
and ensure answers are grounded in the current approved source with an audit trail, while
ensuring accuracy, compliance reporting and member trust are not compromised. The copilot is
advisory and must fail safe, with no impact to core member-servicing systems if unavailable.

User pain points

Difficulty searching quickly across scattered sources, inconsistent answers between agents,
uncertainty about which policy version is current, long onboarding for new agents, and no
audit trail for what was told to a member. These are inferred from the stated functional
requirements and outcomes.

Use case classification

A custom support copilot for member-facing operations, combining several AI use case types:
RAG chatbot, document search and summarisation, decision support, citation-grounded knowledge
grounding, classification/triage of queries, human-in-the-loop review and AI governance and
evaluation capabilities.
